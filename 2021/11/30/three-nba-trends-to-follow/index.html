Three NBA trends to follow

2021-11-30

Similar to my previous post about the MLB, I wanted to take a turn and bring attention to two interesting trends happening in the NBA today, as well as a peek at Dean Oliver’s “Four Factors of Basketball Success.” The two trends I’ll be visualizing look at the main pillar of basketball today: 3-point shooting.

I’ll begin by loading tidyverse, ggrepel, and gt.

library(tidyverse)
library(ggrepel)
library(gt)

The first trend I’ll be showcasing is 3-point attempts (3PA) per game by each team. It’s obvious that the player who catapulted 3-point shooting to it’s throne today is Stephen Curry. I’m interested to see how Curry’s voluminous 3-point shooting has affected the league since the 2014-15 season (his first ring, thanks to a ridiculous 44% on 8.1 attempts from three).

I’ll start with loading in our data.

threepoint <- read_csv("~/Desktop/SPMC 350 Files/homework/data/threepointattempts.csv")

I’m only interested in the 3-point stats. Furthermore, I’ll be mutating two fields. 3Ppct: multiplying by 100 to get a readable percentage. 3Pgame: divides total 3PA by total games to get average 3PA per game.

threepoint <- threepoint %>%
  select(Season, Team, G, `3P`, `3PA`, `3Ppct`) %>%
  mutate(
    `3Ppct` = ((`3Ppct`) * 100),
    `3Pgame` = ((`3PA` / G))) -> threepoint

In order to make a line chart featuring every team individually, I’ll make a new data frame called nba, then use the %in% operator to filter data that matches that criteria (the new nba data frame).

The T-wolves are 1st in the league this season (42.9 3PA/game). Up from LAST in the league in 2014-15 (14.9 3PA/game) – a 287% increase.

Golden State, known for 3-point shooting, was only first in the league once (2015-16). Other seasons they’ve been near the top or buried in the middle. Klay and Steph going down to injury in 2019-20 understandably hurt their production.

The Rockets, on the other hand, were first in the league in five out of the eight seasons. James Harden’s time in H-town led to an absolutely insane amount of shots taken from behind the arc. The Rockets set an unfathomable record 45 3PA/game in back-to-back seasons. With Harden now gone, their attempts have been slashed.

Lastly, I’ll be looking at a different metric. How do basketball teams win games? Dean Oliver identified what he called the “Four Factors of Basketball Success.” Shooting, turnovers, rebounding, and free throws. He weighted them in order of importance: shooting (40%), turnovers (25%), rebounding (20%), and free throws (15%).

Shooting is measured with Effective Field Goal Percentage (eFG%), turnovers are measured with Turnover Percentage (TOV%), rebounding with Offensive and Defensive Rebound Percentage (ORB% and DRB%), and free throws by how often a team gets to the line and how often they make them – Free Throws per Field Goal Attempt (FT/FGA).

Because basketball is an offensive and defensive game, Dean Oliver’s theory can include up to eight factors. First, loading in our data, which has season stats of every NBA championship team since the 1979-80 season (when TOV% was first implemented). Then, only selecting the needed stats.

champs <- read_csv("~/Desktop/SPMC 350 Files/homework/data/champs.csv")

champs <- champs %>%
  select(Season, Team, W, L, Pct, `eFG%`, `TOV%`, `ORB%`, FT_FGA, `OppeFG%`, `OppTOV%`, `OppORB%`, OppFT_FGA)

A table is how I’ll showcase the top 10 Finals champs based on Dean Oliver’s success factors. I’m going to mutate and multiply the eight factors by 100 so I have a readable percentage. Then mutate a net value for each category (Net eFG%, Net TOV%, Net ORB%, and Net FT_FGA). This is so we can accurately see if the teams out-performed their opponents in each category. Lastly, mutating the final metric, FourFactorScore, which takes each net value and multiplies it by its respective weight that Oliver listed, then adding them together.

champs %>%
  mutate(
    `eFG%` = (`eFG%` * 100),           #shifting decimal point so its on a 1-100 scale
    `OppeFG%` = (`OppeFG%` * 100),
    `TOV%` = (`TOV%` * 100),
    `OppTOV%` = (`OppTOV%` * 100),
    `ORB%` = (`ORB%` * 100),
    `OppORB%` = (`OppORB%` * 100),
     FT_FGA = (FT_FGA * 100),
     OppFT_FGA = (OppFT_FGA * 100), 
    `Net_eFG%` = (`eFG%` - `OppeFG%`),      #Net values of each factor
    `Net_TOV%` = (`OppTOV%` - `TOV%`),
    `Net_ORB%` = (`ORB%` - `OppORB%`),
     Net_FT_FGA = (FT_FGA - OppFT_FGA),
     FourFactorScore = (`Net_eFG%` * .40) + (`Net_TOV%` * .25) + (`Net_ORB%` * .20) + (Net_FT_FGA * .15)) -> champsstats

Finally, I’ll select the W/L records and net statistics.

champsstats %>% 
  select(Season, Team, W, L, Pct, `Net_eFG%`, `Net_TOV%`, `Net_ORB%`, Net_FT_FGA, FourFactorScore) -> champstable
